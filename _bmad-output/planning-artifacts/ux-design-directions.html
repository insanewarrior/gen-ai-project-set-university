Compare All Directions

Side-by-side summary of all 6 directions. Each represents a different emphasis: dashboard hub, chat-forward, compact logging, citation showcase, card-heavy, and minimal data.

D1: Dashboard-First

Home as hub — log button + chat input + recent sessions

Strengths: Balanced, surfaces all features, clear entry points
Risk: Could feel cluttered on small screens
Best for: Returning users who want quick overview

D2: Chat-Forward

AI coaching experience front and center

Strengths: Emphasizes the "aha" moment, screenshot-ready
Risk: May underwhelm before enough data
Best for: Showcasing the AI coaching differentiator

D3: Compact Logger

Dense, efficient set entry — Strong-inspired

Strengths: Fastest logging, data-dense, sweaty-hands proof
Risk: Looks complex to beginners
Best for: The daily logging interaction

D4: Citation Showcase

Prominent dual-source citation blocks with labels

Strengths: Most visually impressive citations, highly shareable
Risk: Citation blocks may dominate on short answers
Best for: The "aha" response and social sharing

D5: Card-Heavy

Elevated surfaces, generous spacing, modern feel

Strengths: Polished, clear visual grouping, large touch targets
Risk: Uses more vertical space, more scrolling
Best for: First impressions and onboarding

D6: Minimal Data

Borderless, content-only, pure function

Strengths: Cleanest, most data per screen, feels professional
Risk: May feel too sparse for new users
Best for: Data-heavy athletes who want density

Recommended Combination

Dashboard: D1 layout (balanced hub) with D5 card styling (polished, clear grouping)
Logging Form: D3 compact logger (proven fast pattern, Strong-inspired)
Chat/Citations: D4 citation showcase (prominent dual-source blocks for the "aha" moment)
History: D6 minimal approach (data-dense session list, pure function)

This combination optimizes each screen for its primary purpose: the dashboard invites, the logger captures, the chat impresses, and the history serves.
