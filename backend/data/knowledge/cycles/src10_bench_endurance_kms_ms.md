# SRC10: Bench Press Endurance Cycle (КМС to Master of Sport)

## Overview

SRC10 is the bench press strength-endurance cycle for established athletes at КМС to МС level. The 12-week program emphasizes muscular hypertrophy and multi-rep strength, making it appropriate for off-season preparation when an athlete needs to expand their work capacity and build muscular endurance before returning to peak strength work. It complements the strength cycles (SRC5, SRC6) by addressing the rep range and volume profile they deliberately exclude.

## Target Athlete Profile

КМС to МС level. Body weight minimum 80 kg with optimal weight-to-height ratio. The athlete must have previous competition experience — SRC10 is not a learning cycle for novices. Bench press technique must be well-developed including proper foot drive, scapular retraction, and arch.

No musculoskeletal injuries or contraindications. Access to consistent nutrition and recovery; stable training schedule for the full 12 weeks; the cycle requires uninterrupted training without competitions or major life disruptions during its run.

## Training Structure

Three training sessions per week. Recommended schedule is Monday-Wednesday-Friday or Tuesday-Thursday-Saturday with at least one rest day between sessions. Twelve weeks, twelve microcycles.

The primary repetition range is 8 to 12 reps per set, with occasional 15 to 20 rep work for specific accessory exercises. This is fundamentally different from SRC5/SRC6 which cap at six reps. The higher rep ranges drive hypertrophy and muscular endurance adaptations that the strength cycles don't target.

The cycle is bench-only — squats and deadlifts are not programmed. Athletes who want to maintain squat and deadlift during the bench endurance phase must add separate sessions outside the cycle's structure with self-selected intensity levels. The published guidance suggests keeping these supplementary lifts at light to medium intensity to avoid recovery interference with the cycle's bench-focused work.

## Programming Parameters

Correction percentage default is 0.4% per microcycle, slightly lower than the 0.5% of strength cycles. The lower default reflects the higher absolute volume of an endurance cycle — recovery resources go further with smaller per-microcycle progressions.

The correction percentage can be adjusted based on response: increase to 0.5 to 0.6% if the first 3 to 4 weeks tolerate the load well, or reduce if fatigue accumulates. Pharmacology adjustments follow standard LMS rules.

## Rest Periods

Rest between sets varies based on exercise difficulty. Brief intervals are used on manageable loads (warm-up sets, lighter accessories). Up to 5 to 7 minutes of rest is appropriate on high-intensity working sets where the athlete is approaching the upper end of the 8 to 12 rep range with weights that fully challenge that rep range. The published instruction explicitly says to avoid excessive muscle pump — pump that limits subsequent sets indicates the rest periods are too short for the prescribed work.

## Implementation Steps

Evaluate athlete parameters against eligibility criteria. Download cycle10.xlsm. Review exercise selection and confirm technical competency for each. Input bench press 1RM with 5 kg accuracy estimate. Schedule training days across the 12 weeks. Save the customized file and begin.

## Post-Cycle Progression

After SRC10, the recommended next step is a strength cycle. The 12 weeks of endurance work expand the athlete's work capacity, which then becomes the foundation for higher absolute intensities in a subsequent strength block. The natural progression is SRC10 → SRC5 or SRC6 (depending on level) → block cycles for peaking.

## Why Endurance Phases Matter for Strength Athletes

The LMS system's inclusion of endurance cycles (SRC3, SRC10, SRC11, SRC16) reflects a deliberate periodization philosophy: pure strength work in the 1 to 6 rep range produces neuromuscular gains but does not maximize the muscle size and connective tissue adaptations that support long-term strength. Periodically inserting an 8 to 12 rep cycle gives the muscle time to grow and the tendons time to thicken, which then enables higher absolute strength when the athlete returns to the strength rep range. Skipping the endurance phases entirely produces athletes who plateau early because their strength has outgrown their tissue capacity.
