# SRC7: Peak Powerlifting Cycle (Master of Sport to International Master of Sport)

## Overview

SRC7 is the elite-level peaking cycle in the LMS powerlifting series. The 12-week program targets athletes at МС to МСМК (International Master of Sport) classification in the raw division and is designed to produce a peak strength state for competition or formal max attempts in week 13. Unlike SRC1 and SRC2 which build strength across the macrocycle, SRC7 sharpens an already-built strength base into a competition-ready peak.

## Target Athlete Profile

МС or МСМК level confirmed by competition results. Body weight 70 kg or more — meaningfully lower minimum than the 80 kg threshold of SRC1/SRC2 because elite athletes at lighter classes still benefit from the cycle's peaking structure. Weight-to-height ratio must remain in normal proportions.

The training goal must be balanced strength gains across all three lifts. SRC7 is not for athletes who want to specialize on a single lift while neglecting another — its load distribution assumes the athlete is preparing all three for competition.

Health status: no significant musculoskeletal issues or injuries requiring immediate treatment. Recovery capacity must include access to recovery measures as needed: dietary modifications, massage, active recovery work. The cycle assumes the athlete has the resources to support five high-intensity weekly sessions.

The cycle's documentation is explicit that it presupposes either quality nutrition with proper sleep or anabolic support. SRC7 is calibrated for high-output recovery; a natural athlete with marginal recovery resources should consider SRC2 instead.

## Training Structure

Five training days per week — denser than the other LMS powerlifting cycles. Recommended schedule is Monday-Tuesday-Thursday-Friday-Saturday. Twelve weeks, twelve microcycles, plus a 13th week reserved for competition or formal attempts.

The cycle employs an intensive loading scheme with high-percentage work. Warm-ups and all working sets maintain six repetitions or fewer per set — a strict cap because the loads are heavy enough that higher-rep work would compromise recovery. Light training sessions within the microcycle incorporate sub-maximal volumes to preserve neural freshness without adding fatigue.

## Optional Deadlift Specialization on Day 5

The cycle includes optional deadlift variations on the fifth training day depending on the athlete's pulling style. Sumo pullers perform a dead-stop variation at the knee with a 1 to 2 second pause — building strength out of the lockout transition. Conventional pullers perform deficit pulls from a 5 cm box — adding range of motion to strengthen the bottom position of the pull.

These optional fifth-day variations are not arbitrary additions; they target the part of each pulling style most prone to weakness in elite athletes preparing for competition.

## Implementation Steps

Verify baseline 1RM in all three lifts with 5 kg precision. Download cycle7.xlsm and enable editing. Review the selected exercises for technical competency. Confirm 12 consecutive competition-free weeks are available before the planned competition or attempt. Input body weight and establish the adjustment percentage (0.5 default, modifiable). Schedule training: Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday.

## Competition Timing

Week 13 accommodates competition or formal attempts following cycle completion. The cycle is calibrated to produce peak readiness in this window — going beyond week 13 risks deloading off the peak; cutting short before week 12 risks insufficient peaking volume.

## Distinction from SRC2

SRC2 is a strength-building cycle for КМС-МС lifters; SRC7 is a peaking cycle for МС-МСМК lifters. Different goals, different load math, different daily distribution. SRC7 assumes the athlete already has the strength and is sharpening it; SRC2 assumes the athlete is still acquiring it. Running SRC7 on a lifter who is not yet МС-level will produce overload because the absolute intensities are calibrated for athletes whose 1RM percentages translate to absolute weights their bodies can recover from at five sessions per week.
