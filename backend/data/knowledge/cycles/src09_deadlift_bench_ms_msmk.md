# SRC9: Deadlift and Bench Cycle (Master of Sport to International Master of Sport)

## Overview

SRC9 is the LMS deadlift-and-bench specialization cycle for elite athletes at МС to МСМК level. The 12-week mixed cycle combines pure strength methods with peak-reaching approaches, making it suitable for both regular training periods and direct competition preparation. It is the cycle of choice for an elite powerlifter whose deadlift exceeds their squat significantly and who wants to maintain or improve bench while bringing the pull to its peak.

## Target Athlete Profile

МС or МСМК level confirmed by competition results. Body weight 80 kg or more. Deadlift performance must significantly exceed squat performance — this asymmetry is the cycle's core assumption. An athlete whose squat is similar to or stronger than their deadlift will find SRC9 misallocated; they should run SRC2 or SRC7 instead.

No acute musculoskeletal injuries requiring immediate treatment. Adequate recovery capacity is essential, and the athlete's job must not impose significant physical fatigue on top of the training load.

## Two Variants

SRC9 ships in two variants matched to the athlete's pulling style. SRC9k (cycle9k.xlsm) is for classical (conventional) deadlift technique. SRC9s (cycle9s.xlsm) is for sumo deadlift technique. The cycles have the same overall structure but the deadlift variations and accessories differ to match the muscular emphasis of each style — sumo loads quads and adductors more, conventional loads spinal erectors and hamstrings more.

The athlete must commit to one variant for the full 12 weeks. Switching styles mid-cycle disrupts the load math because the prescribed weights are calibrated against the chosen style's 1RM.

## Training Structure

Twelve weeks organized into 12 microcycles. Four sessions per week, optimally Monday-Tuesday-Thursday-Friday. The cycle uses a strength-period framework — warm-ups and all working sets stay at six repetitions or fewer to avoid excessive fatigue accumulation.

The cycle implements both pure strength methods (max-effort-style top sets) and peak-reaching approaches (intensity waves that mirror peaking structure). This combination makes SRC9 work as both a base strength cycle and as a competition lead-in — the athlete can run it 12 weeks before a meet and arrive at peak readiness.

## Programming Parameters

Correction percentage default is 0.5%. The pharmacology adjustments follow the standard LMS rules: lower for naturals, higher for athletes on cycle.

The athlete inputs accurate 1RM values for deadlift and bench press with 5 kg precision. Squat is not directly programmed in SRC9 — the cycle assumes the athlete maintains squat through their own supplementary work or accepts a temporary squat de-emphasis during the 12-week pull-and-press focus.

## Implementation Steps

Download the appropriate variant (cycle9k.xlsm for classical, cycle9s.xlsm for sumo). Enable macros and editing. Confirm technical proficiency in all prescribed exercises. Input 1RM values for deadlift and bench. Set the weekly schedule and training dates. Configure the correction percentage from the reference table. Begin training. Use six or fewer reps during all warm-ups to avoid the fatigue accumulation that higher-rep warm-ups produce.

## When to Use SRC9 vs SRC7

SRC7 is the balanced peaking cycle for elite powerlifters who train all three lifts symmetrically. SRC9 is the deadlift-and-bench specialization cycle for elite lifters whose deadlift dominates. Choose SRC7 if you want all three lifts maximally peaked; choose SRC9 if your deadlift is your strongest lift and you want to push it further while protecting the bench. Running SRC9 on a balanced lifter will under-emphasize the squat to a degree that may compromise three-lift competition totals.

## Post-Cycle Progression

After SRC9, the natural progression is a competition (the cycle is configured to produce peak readiness in week 13) or a transition into a different specialization cycle (e.g., a squat-focused phase if the squat has fallen behind during the pull-and-press emphasis).
