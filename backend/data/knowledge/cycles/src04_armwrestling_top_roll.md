# SRC4: Arm Wrestling Top-Roll Cycle

## Overview

SRC4 is the dedicated arm wrestling cycle in the LMS system, authored by Andrey Barskov. It is a 12-week strength training cycle for top-roll-style arm wrestlers at II adult rank through Candidate to Master of Sport. The cycle structure differs from the powerlifting cycles in two important ways: more training days per week (four), and an exercise vocabulary built around arm wrestling movement patterns rather than barbell lifts.

## Target Athlete Profile

The cycle is for top-roll specialists — athletes who attack with hand and wrist leverage rather than the closing-elbow hook style. Hook-style arm wrestlers will find SRC4 misallocated for their needs.

Body weight minimum 80 kg with a normal weight-to-height ratio. Required technical skill: established proficiency in pronation, brachioradialis loading, and wrist abduction patterns. The cycle assumes none of these are weak points; if they are, isolated remediation work must precede the cycle.

The cycle requires equipment that not all gyms provide: an adjustable cable machine, special handles including a "corner" handle for top-roll simulation, and a judo belt. Without these, the prescribed exercises cannot be executed properly.

## Training Structure

Four training days per week, optimally Monday-Tuesday-Thursday-Saturday or Tuesday-Wednesday-Friday-Sunday. Twelve weeks, twelve microcycles. The cycle is a strength period — warm-up, cool-down, and main working sets are limited to six repetitions or fewer.

The cycle features wave-loading (undulating periodization within and across microcycles) and micro-weight work (small per-session load increments because arm wrestling muscles are small enough that 1 kg increments are meaningful).

## Programming Parameters

Correction percentage default is 0.5% per microcycle, adjustable. The 1RM input precision is tighter than barbell cycles — 1 to 3 kg is the desired accuracy because the absolute loads in arm-specific exercises are small enough that even 1 kg matters.

## Exercise Vocabulary

The arm-specific exercises in SRC4 do not have direct barbell-sport equivalents. Top imitation is a dumbbell-and-elbow-pad simulation of the top-roll finishing motion. Side press is a horizontal lateral push mimicking hook-style finishing. Pronation with strength bands and supination drills isolate the rotational strength of the forearm. Wrist with strength bands trains wrist flexion against band tension. Shoulder adduction brings the working arm toward the puller's body. Cable lat pull-down builds pulling strength for hook-style and as general posterior chain support. Weighted chin-ups develop the supinated-grip pulling strength used at the table.

Bench press, squat, and deadlift appear at light or medium intensity as background general physical preparation, never as primary stimulus.

## Implementation Steps

Verify the athlete meets all nine criteria documented in the cycle's instructions (style, level, body weight, technical proficiency in the specific patterns, equipment access, no current injuries, adequate recovery). Download cycle4.xlsm. Assess personal maximums in each exercise (1 to 3 kg precision). Verify a 12-week competition-free window. Input personalized parameters and the 0.5% correction percentage.

## Detailed Exercise Prescriptions

The detailed per-microcycle exercise prescriptions, set/rep schemes, and intensity progressions are encoded in the cycle4.xlsm spreadsheet itself. The instructional page describes the framework; the spreadsheet contains the executable program. The structural pattern visible from inspecting the spreadsheet: each microcycle distributes work across four classes — heavy top imitation work on one day, side press emphasis on another, pulling work on a third, and wrist/forearm specialization on the fourth, with light variations of each pattern interspersed.

## Post-Cycle Progression

After SRC4, the typical progression is to a peaking phase before competition or to repeat the cycle with updated 1RM inputs. There is currently no LMS Master of Sport to International Master of Sport-level arm wrestling cycle published, so elite arm wrestlers either iterate SRC4 with progressively heavier inputs or design custom programming.
