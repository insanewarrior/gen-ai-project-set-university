# SRC6: Bench Press Strength Cycle (КМС to Master of Sport)

## Overview

SRC6 is the bench press strength cycle for higher-level athletes — the КМС to МС counterpart to SRC5. The 12-week cycle is structured for four training sessions per week with intensified loading and a peak-strength-style split on heavy days for greater load differentiation. It is the cycle of choice for an established bench press specialist who has outgrown SRC5 but is not yet ready for the elite-level SRC10/SRC11 endurance variants or the bench-block cycles for peaking.

## Target Athlete Profile

КМС to МС level. Body weight 80 kg or more. Raw (unequipped) division. The athlete must have established bench press technique at a level where minor refinements during the cycle are sufficient — major technical reconstruction is incompatible with the cycle's intensity demands.

Adequate recovery capacity is essential. The cycle's intensified loading consumes more recovery resources than SRC5's, so insufficient sleep, nutrition, or psychological recovery will compromise progress. Caloric surplus nutrition is required. No significant musculoskeletal injuries requiring immediate treatment.

## Training Structure

Twelve consecutive weeks of training. Four sessions per week, optimally Monday-Tuesday-Thursday-Friday or Tuesday-Wednesday-Friday-Saturday. The cycle uses traditional load organization combined with intensity principles, plus partial implementation of high-volume training and micro-weight training.

Heavy training days within each microcycle include peak-strength-exit-period-style splits for load differentiation. This means that on heavy bench days, the working sets are organized in waves that mirror the structure of a peaking-phase session — multiple intensity steps within the same workout — rather than a single uniform working weight. This produces both higher peak intensity and longer average time under tension within a single session.

## Programming Parameters

Default correction percentage is 0.5% per microcycle. The cycle is calibrated for natural КМС-МС-level athletes; pharmacology corrections follow the same rules as other LMS cycles (negative or zero for athletes coming off, higher for athletes on cycle).

## Setup and Implementation

Download cycle6.xlsm. Enable macros and editing. Verify athlete meets all eligibility conditions. Review the prescribed exercises and confirm technical capability for each. Examine the cool-down exercise recommendations in the first microcycle's comment column. Confirm 12 weeks of competition-free training are available. Input 1RM values for bench press and supplementary lifts (5 kg precision). Assign training days. Set the correction percentage. Save and begin.

## Post-Cycle Progression

After SRC6, the recommended next phase is a peak strength exit period or a speed advancement period. The peak strength path leads into a competition or a max-attempt session; the speed path addresses neural-velocity adaptations that the strength cycle's volume work does not directly target. Athletes who maintain a steady training calendar without immediate competitions can also rerun SRC6 with updated 1RM inputs after a brief deload, building progressively higher absolute strength across multiple iterations.

## Why SRC6 Differs from SRC5

The two cycles share a four-day-per-week, 12-week structure but differ in intensity profile and load math. SRC5 calibrates for athletes still building toward elite intensity; its volume serves as a base-building phase. SRC6 assumes the athlete already has that base and emphasizes intensity differentiation within the microcycle, with heavier peaks and more aggressive load progression. Choosing the wrong cycle for the athlete's actual level produces predictable failure modes — SRC5 underloads an МС lifter; SRC6 grinds a II-rank lifter into the ground.
