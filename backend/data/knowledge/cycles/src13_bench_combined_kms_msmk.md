# SRC13: Combined Strength Bench Cycle (Candidate to Master of Sport to International Master of Sport)

## Overview

SRC13 is an experimental 12-week bench press cycle that combines mixed periodization — 5 to 6 rep work alongside low-rep sets (2 to 3 reps and singles) — with high intensity and linear tonnage progression. The cycle is designed for advanced lifters across a wide level range (Candidate to Master of Sport through International Master of Sport) who can handle aggressive load progression with adequate recovery support. Unlike SRC10/SRC11 which are pure endurance cycles or SRC5/SRC6 which are pure strength cycles, SRC13 deliberately mixes both rep ranges within the same macrocycle.

## Target Athlete Profile

Candidate to Master of Sport through International Master of Sport level. The cycle accepts a wider competitive level range than most LMS cycles because its mixed periodization scales reasonably across the Candidate to Master of Sport-to-International Master of Sport continuum — a Candidate to Master of Sport lifter and an International Master of Sport lifter both have absolute intensities and recovery capacities that the load math can accommodate, though the absolute weights differ enormously.

Advanced technical level is required. Athletes must have established technique with continuous technical refinement happening as part of regular training. The cycle does not budget time for major technical reconstruction.

No significant injuries — athletes with apparent musculoskeletal issues or injuries requiring immediate treatment cannot use SRC13. Adequate recovery capacity is essential. The cycle demands sports-level nutrition and recovery; it is unsuitable for athletes with high work-related physical demands. The aggressive load progression compounds fatigue rapidly when recovery is marginal.

## Training Structure

Four training sessions per week, optimally Monday-Tuesday-Thursday-Friday. Twelve weeks, twelve microcycles.

The mixed periodization is the cycle's distinguishing feature. Within each microcycle, some sessions emphasize 5 to 6 rep work (typical strength-period range) while others drop to 2 to 3 reps or singles (peak-style work). The combination produces both the volume stimulus of moderate-rep strength work and the neural intensity of low-rep peaking work without sequencing them across the macrocycle.

Linear tonnage increase across the macrocycle drives progressive overload. Each microcycle's total tonnage is meaningfully higher than the prior microcycle's, producing the aggressive load progression that defines the cycle.

## Important Constraints

The cycle is bench-only — there is no programmed squat or deadlift work. The cycle's annotation explicitly notes the absence of back and leg loading and recommends athletes add their own light supplementary work for those patterns if they want to maintain them.

Recommended cool-down sequence: lateral and front shoulder raises, tricep cable pushdowns, leg extensions or curls (light supplementary leg work), light squats or hyperextensions, machine chest work and cable crossovers, and rotator cuff strengthening. The cool-down is not mandatory but is recommended because the cycle's bench-only structure leaves the rest of the body underexposed without it.

Stretching recommendations: shoulders and arms after press days, bridge stretching on day two of the microcycle.

## Programming Parameters

Correction percentage default is 0.5%, adjusted weekly based on the reference table in the spreadsheet. The aggressive linear tonnage increase means correction percentage adjustments matter more in SRC13 than in milder cycles — small over-corrections compound across the 12 weeks and can produce overload by week 8 or 9.

Warm-ups stay at six reps maximum to avoid fatigue accumulation that would compromise the working sets.

## Implementation Steps

Download cycle13.xlsm. Enable macros and editing. Review all exercises and confirm technical capability. Examine cool-down exercises listed in the first microcycle's comments. Ensure 12-week competition-free period. Input 1RM bench press value (estimated to nearest 5 kg). Select training days, optimally Monday-Tuesday-Thursday-Friday. Set the correction percentage. Save and begin.

## Why "Experimental"

The LMS documentation describes SRC13 as experimental because mixed periodization within a single macrocycle is uncommon in their catalog — most cycles are either strength-period or endurance-period purebreeds. The combination produces results when it works (the data backing the cycle showed strength gains across the Candidate to Master of Sport through International Master of Sport range), but it places higher demands on the athlete's ability to recover from heterogeneous stimuli within the same week. Athletes for whom the cycle does not produce expected progress may need to revert to single-period cycles (SRC6 or SRC11) where the recovery profile is more predictable.

## Post-Cycle Progression

After SRC13, the typical progression is either a competition (the cycle leaves the athlete near peak readiness) or a deload-and-restart with updated 1RM inputs. The mixed-period nature of the cycle means it does not require a specific complementary cycle the way pure strength or pure endurance cycles do; SRC13 covers more of the periodization landscape on its own.
