# SRC16: Bench Press Endurance Cycle for Two Training Days per Week (II rank to КМС)

## Overview

SRC16 is the LMS bench press strength-endurance cycle for athletes limited to two training days per week. It is the endurance counterpart to SRC15 — same target athlete profile (bench press specialists at II rank to КМС, body weight 60 kg+, two-day-per-week schedule constraint), same 12-week duration, but with multi-rep strength-endurance work instead of heavy peak strength loading. The cycle is the second half of the SRC15+SRC16 pairing recommended for athletes whose schedule supports only two training days.

## Target Athlete Profile

Bench press specialists of beginner to intermediate level (II rank through КМС) with demanding work schedules. Body weight minimum 60 kg. No acute injuries. Adequate squatting technique with a deep squat position recommended (because SRC16 includes squat work as background lower-body maintenance — the deep position is needed for the prescribed range of motion).

Commitment to proper nutrition and recovery is essential. The cycle's volume accumulates fatigue across the 12 weeks; insufficient recovery resources stall progression. No intense physical labor on non-training days because the strength-endurance volume requires recovery rather than accumulated depletion.

## Training Structure

Two training sessions per week, spaced optimally. Recommended schedules: Monday-Thursday or Tuesday-Friday. Consecutive training days are explicitly avoided — even with the lighter loads of an endurance cycle, the volume requires recovery windows between sessions.

Twelve weeks, twelve microcycles. The focus is multi-rep strength endurance, combining light sessions with heavy near-failure sets within each microcycle. The published instruction specifies "not fewer than 8 repetitions in warm-up or cool-down work" — every set across the cycle stays in the strength-endurance range.

## Programming Parameters

Default correction percentage is 0.5% for natural athletes, with a maximum of 2.5% under aggressive progression conditions. The same adjustment options apply across the LMS cycles. The Excel cell for the correction percentage is AH58 — the documentation calls this out explicitly because adjusting this single parameter is the primary tuning knob across the cycle.

## Setup Instructions

Download cycle16.xlsm (requires Microsoft Excel). Enable editing and active content. Navigate to the "1 в день" (Day 1) sheet for prescribed daily work. Set actual start dates in the Date column. Input maxima for squat and bench press in the yellow-highlighted cells using realistic values. Set the correction percentage in cell AH58.

## Important Guidelines

Rest periods are flexible, governed by perceived difficulty rather than fixed times. On lighter sets, brief rests are sufficient. On heavier near-failure sets, longer rests preserve form and quality across the prescribed working volume.

Include flexibility work for shoulders and chest after each session. Practice bridge holds — these maintain spinal mobility and chest opening, which the heavy bench work would otherwise gradually restrict. The cycle's instructions specifically mention bridge work as a complementary practice.

Follow the prescribed volume and intensity exactly. The strength-endurance cycles are calibrated for cumulative effect; deviating from the prescription within a single microcycle does not significantly impact the cycle, but persistent deviation across multiple microcycles invalidates the load math.

No intense additional exercises on rest days. The two non-training days each week are for recovery; substantial physical work on those days defeats the purpose of the low-frequency structure.

## Why Pair with SRC15

The LMS catalog pairs SRC15 (peak strength, two days/week) and SRC16 (strength endurance, two days/week) as complementary cycles. Running them sequentially produces a 24-week macrostructure with periodized variability that the two-day-per-week schedule constraint would otherwise prevent. After 24 weeks of alternating peak strength and strength endurance, retest the maximum to assess accumulated progress.

The pairing rationale: peak strength cycles produce neuromuscular gains but do not maximize tissue adaptation; strength-endurance cycles produce tissue adaptation but do not maximize neural expression. Alternating them captures both adaptation paths within a constraint that, if pursued single-mindedly, would limit one or the other.

## Distinction from SRC10/SRC11

SRC10 and SRC11 are bench press endurance cycles for three-or-four-day schedules at КМС-МС and МС-МСМК levels respectively. SRC16 is the lighter-weight-class, lower-level, two-day-per-week variant. Running SRC10 on a two-day schedule would compress the volume into per-session loads that exceed recovery capacity; running SRC16 on a four-day schedule would underload the athlete. The right cycle for the right schedule is essential.

## Post-Cycle Progression

After SRC16, the recommended progression is back to SRC15 (peak strength on two days/week) for the next 12 weeks, completing the 24-week alternation cycle. Athletes whose schedule expands to three or four days can transition to SRC5/SRC6 (strength) or SRC10/SRC11 (endurance) with updated 1RM inputs.
