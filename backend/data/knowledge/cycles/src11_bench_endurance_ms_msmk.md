# SRC11: Bench Press Endurance Cycle (Master of Sport to International Master of Sport)

## Overview

SRC11 is the elite-level bench press strength-endurance cycle in the LMS series. It is the МС-МСМК counterpart to SRC10 — same 8 to 20 rep philosophy and same 12-week duration, but calibrated for the absolute intensities and recovery demands of master-level athletes. The cycle is appropriate for elite benchers who have completed strength cycles (SRC6 or block cycles) and need to expand work capacity, drive hypertrophy, and refine technique under sub-maximal sustained loading before returning to peak strength work.

## Target Athlete Profile

МС to МСМК level. Body weight minimum 80 kg with near-optimal height-to-weight ratio. Confirmed competition experience — the cycle assumes the athlete has competed and has a reliable 1RM history that drives the load math. Advanced technical proficiency in the bench press and supplementary lifts. No musculoskeletal injuries or limitations. Stable lifestyle with adequate nutrition capacity.

A confident caloric surplus and sufficient protein intake are explicitly required. The cycle's loading consumes meaningful glycogen and produces substantial muscle damage; insufficient calories or protein produce predictable failure modes (stalled progress, accumulated fatigue, soft-tissue overuse).

## Training Structure

Three training days per week. Recommended schedule is Monday-Wednesday-Friday or Tuesday-Thursday-Saturday. The cycle's documentation also notes a denser variant with 4 sessions weekly (Mon-Tue-Thu-Fri or Tue-Wed-Fri-Sat) for athletes with high recovery capacity. Twelve weeks, twelve microcycles.

The microcycle includes two squat sessions and one deadlift session as background lower-body work, integrated to maintain the squat and deadlift during the bench-focused phase rather than letting them detrain entirely. This is a meaningful difference from SRC10, which leaves squats and deadlifts entirely to the athlete's discretion.

The primary repetition range is 8 to 12 reps for main exercises. Occasional 15 to 20 rep work appears for specific finishers. The cycle's documentation makes this explicit: "lifts should not drop below 8 reps in main exercises or finishers" — the entire cycle stays in the strength-endurance range, with no max-effort or low-rep work.

## Programming Parameters

The default correction percentage is 0.5%. Adjustment guidelines: if the first 3 to 4 weeks are tolerated well, increase to 0.6 to 0.8% or higher. For athletes early in a steroid cycle, 1% or higher is realistic. For athletes on a bridge or in post-cycle therapy, use 0% or negative values.

The correction percentage can be adjusted weekly based on subjective recovery — this responsiveness is more important in endurance cycles than in strength cycles because the volume accumulates fatigue over weeks rather than within sessions.

Rest periods: adequate recovery between sets, avoiding excessive muscle pump. The range is short breaks for easy sets up to 5 to 7 minutes for heavy sets in the 8 to 12 rep range with substantial weight.

## Implementation Steps

Verify the athlete meets all correspondence conditions. Download cycle11.xlsm. Enable Excel editing and macros when prompted. Review prescribed exercises and confirm technical capability. Check cool-down exercise recommendations in the first microcycle's comments. Confirm 12 weeks of competition-free training are available. Customize: input 1RM values for bench, squat, and deadlift with 5 kg precision; schedule training days and dates; set the correction percentage at 0.5% default. Save and begin.

## Distinction from SRC10

SRC10 targets КМС-МС-level athletes with 8-12 rep work centered on the bench press, leaving squat and deadlift to the athlete. SRC11 targets МС-МСМК-level athletes with the same rep philosophy but integrated lower-body work to keep the full powerlifting profile intact. The difference is not just intensity — it is structural. An МС-level athlete running SRC10 may find their squat and deadlift detraining too far over 12 weeks; an МС-level athlete running SRC11 maintains the full lift profile.

## Post-Cycle Progression

After SRC11, the natural progression is a strength cycle (block cycles for benchers, SRC6 if the athlete needs another iteration of the strength rep range, or directly into a peaking cycle if competition is approaching). The work capacity built during SRC11 becomes the foundation for the strength cycle's higher intensities.
