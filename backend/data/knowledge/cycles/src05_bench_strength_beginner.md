# SRC5: Bench Press Strength Cycle (II rank to КМС)

## Overview

SRC5 is the LMS strength-period cycle for bench press specialists at II rank through Candidate Master of Sport. The 12-week, four-day-per-week structure emphasizes bench press development as the primary lift while giving meaningful attention to the standing press as a secondary specialty. It is the bench-focused counterpart to SRC1's three-lift powerlifting structure.

## Target Athlete Profile

The cycle requires body weight of 80 kg or more with appropriate proportions. The documented height ceilings are: maximum 174 cm at 80 kg body weight, maximum 180 cm at 100 kg. Athletes taller than these thresholds at the corresponding weights have unfavorable leverages for the cycle's load assumptions and should choose a different program.

Raw (unequipped) division competitors only. The athlete must have no pronounced weak points in the bench press movement — if there is a clear hole in the lift (off-the-chest weakness, lockout weakness, asymmetric press), it should be addressed before the cycle. The standing press technique must already be established because SRC5 trains it as a meaningful secondary lift, not a teaching context.

The cycle assumes the athlete can provide adequate regime and recovery — not associated with burdensome, excessively emotional, or physically difficult work. Caloric surplus with adequate protein intake is required.

## Training Structure

Four training days per week, optimally Monday-Tuesday-Thursday-Friday or Tuesday-Wednesday-Friday-Saturday. Twelve weeks, twelve microcycles. Strength-period dosing limits warm-up, preparatory, and other sets to six repetitions or fewer.

The cycle uses a traditional load-dosing scheme with selective high-intensity work. The high-volume principle is partially implemented; micro-loading is not used. Heavy, medium, and light bench press sessions appear within each microcycle, distributed across the four training days so that no single session overloads the bench pattern.

## Standing Press Integration

The standing press is the cycle's secondary specialty. Unlike SRC1 where the standing press is purely accessory, SRC5 dedicates programming time to develop the lift seriously. This makes SRC5 a useful cycle for athletes preparing for events that include the standing press as a contested lift (some federations include it) or for benchers whose lockout would benefit from heavier overhead work.

## Implementation Steps

Confirm athlete meets all correspondence conditions. Download cycle5.xlsm. Enable macros and editing. Review prescribed exercises against personal technical capability. Identify cool-down exercises listed in the comment column of the spreadsheet. Confirm 12-week competition-free window. Customize: input 1RM values with 5 kg precision in the yellow fields. Assign training days and dates. Set the correction percentage (default 0.5). Save and begin.

## Post-Cycle Progression

After SRC5, the recommended next step is a peak strength period or a speed development period. The peak strength path is appropriate for athletes preparing for competition; the speed development path is appropriate when the athlete needs to address rate-of-force-development deficits revealed during the strength cycle.
