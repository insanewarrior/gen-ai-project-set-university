# SRC1: Powerlifting Strength Cycle (II rank to Candidate Master of Sport)

## Overview

SRC1 is the foundational self-calculating cycle for powerlifters in the Bondarenko/LMS system. It is a 12-week strength period for athletes at Russian II adult rank, I adult rank, or Candidate Master of Sport (КМС) level. The cycle assumes raw (unequipped) competition and an established technical base. Three training sessions per week with one or two days of rest between sessions form the macrocycle structure.

## Target Athlete Profile

The cycle requires a body weight of 80 kg or heavier with a normal weight-to-height ratio. Athletes lighter than 80 kg should look at SRC14 instead because the loading assumptions in SRC1 are calibrated for the heavier body class. The athlete must have established technical proficiency in the squat, bench press, and deadlift — minor refinements during the cycle are acceptable but the major technical patterns must be in place before starting.

The cycle was originally designed for male lifters; female lifters and lighter male lifters can adapt the cycle but should not run it without modifications. Athletes already within 12 weeks of a competition should not use SRC1 — choose a peaking-oriented cycle (SRC7 or block cycles) instead.

## Training Structure

Twelve consecutive weeks divided into 12 microcycles, each containing three training sessions. The recommended weekly schedules are Monday-Wednesday-Friday or Tuesday-Thursday-Saturday so that one or two rest days separate every session.

The cycle is a strength period (силовой период), meaning the work is dosed according to the classical Bondarenko strength scheme: warm-up sets, accessory work, and cool-down work all stay at six repetitions or fewer. Higher rep work is deliberately excluded because the cycle is calibrated for neuromuscular adaptation rather than hypertrophy.

The load distribution implements partial high-volume training principles — the volume is meaningful but not extreme. The cycle does not implement micro-loading (small per-session weight increments). Selective high-intensity work appears across the macrocycle.

## Exercise Assumptions

SRC1 makes three implicit assumptions about the athlete's biomechanics that drive exercise selection. The squat stance is narrow or medium — wide-stance squatters need a different cycle because the prescribed accessories (front squat, narrow squat) won't transfer optimally. The bench press grip is wide or medium — close-grip specialists are not the target. The deadlift style can be conventional or sumo, both work.

The accessory selection treats leg development as the primary weak point, using wide-stance squats and front squats to address strength imbalances while assuming relatively well-developed back musculature. If your weak point is back rather than legs, the cycle will not directly address it.

## Programming Parameters

The default correction coefficient is 0.5% per week applied to maximal lifts (PM). This means the prescribed load increases by 0.5% of the input PM each microcycle. The athlete may adjust this in the Excel file based on circumstances:

For natural athletes who are coming off a steroid cycle into post-cycle therapy, set the correction to negative values (-0.5% or lower) for a controlled retreat from peak strength. For athletes currently using anabolic steroids, 2% or higher per-week progression is realistic. The 0.5% default is the centerline for natural intermediate athletes.

## Contraindications

SRC1 is unsuitable for novice lifters lacking technical competency, lifters under 80 kg without a significant strength base, athletes with underdeveloped muscle mass relative to height, lifters who require wide-stance squats or narrow-grip presses as their primary lifts, and competitors within 12 weeks of a competition date.

## Setup Steps

The athlete downloads cycle1.xlsm, enables Excel macros and editing, reviews the prescribed exercises against personal technical capability, confirms a 12-week competition-free window, and inputs configuration: current 1RM values for squat/bench/deadlift in yellow cells (5 kg precision is sufficient), training day dates, and the correction percentage (0.5 default).

## Post-Cycle Progression

Three options after the 12 weeks complete: immediate retest of 1RM via maximum attempts; add approximately 10% to the calculated maxima and rerun the cycle for another 12 weeks; or transition to a different training phase such as endurance work, speed development, or peaking. The cycle is repeatable across multiple iterations as long as the athlete continues to make progress.
