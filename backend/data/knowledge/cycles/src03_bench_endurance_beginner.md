# SRC3: Bench Press Strength-Endurance Cycle (Beginner)

## Overview

SRC3 is the LMS system's beginner bench press cycle focused on strength endurance rather than absolute strength. The 12-week cycle uses an 8 to 20 repetition range to build muscular endurance and refine bench press technique under sub-maximal loading. It is the appropriate first structured cycle for an athlete who has been bench pressing informally and now wants a programmatic approach before progressing to dedicated strength work.

## Target Athlete Profile

Beginner or novice bench press athlete. The cycle requires a minimum body weight of 70 kg (lower than the 80 kg minimum for most other LMS cycles), an optimal or near-optimal weight-to-height ratio, adequate athletic predisposition, and good baseline health with no injuries or chronic conditions.

The cycle is unsuitable for athletes using anabolic steroids or in post-cycle therapy — its 1 to 3 percent correction range and modest absolute loads are designed for natural athletes. It is also unsuitable for athletes with musculoskeletal injuries or for those with demanding or heavy work obligations that compromise recovery.

## Training Structure

Three training days per week. Optimal schedule: Monday-Wednesday-Friday or Tuesday-Thursday-Saturday so each session is followed by 24 to 72 hours of recovery. The 12-week duration matches the standard LMS macrocycle.

Repetition range is 8 to 20 reps per set across the cycle's exercises. The bench press appears at the lower end of this range; supplementary work appears at the higher end. Rest between sets is moderate — the athlete should not become overly fatigued or achieve a strong "pump" effect. Excessive pump indicates the rest is too short for the prescribed work. Session duration is capped at 2 hours total.

## Programming Characteristics

The correction coefficient ranges from 1 to 3 percent per microcycle, with 1% the recommended starting point. This is meaningfully higher than the 0.5% default in strength-period cycles because endurance work allows faster nominal load progression — the loads are sub-maximal so larger relative jumps are sustainable.

The cycle's main objective is constant improvement of technique on the bench press and supplementary exercises. The lighter loading characteristic of the endurance period gives the athlete bandwidth to focus on technical refinement that would not be possible at peak intensity.

## Contraindications

Cannot be applied to steroid-using athletes (the cycle's load math assumes natural recovery), athletes with damage to the musculoskeletal system or active injuries, or athletes whose work or lifestyle prevents adequate recovery.

## Implementation Steps

Assess athlete parameters against the eligibility criteria. Download cycle3.xlsm and enable Excel macros. Input 1RM bench press value with 5 kg precision. Establish the weekly training schedule. Adjust the correction coefficient weekly based on subjective recovery — increase it if the previous week felt easy, decrease it if recovery was insufficient.

## Post-Cycle Progression

After SRC3, the recommended next step is to organize a strength cycle. Block-based SRC programs for bench press are the typical bridge from SRC3 (endurance) into peak strength work. The athlete who completes SRC3 successfully has built the muscular endurance and technical foundation to handle the higher intensities of a strength cycle.
