# SRC14: Powerlifting Cycle for Lighter Weight Classes (II rank to Master of Sport)

## Overview

SRC14 is the LMS powerlifting cycle calibrated for athletes in lighter weight classes. The 12-week cycle scales across a wide level range (II rank through Master of Sport) and is designed for body weights from 60 kg upward, lower than the 80 kg minimum of SRC1 and SRC2. The cycle covers all three powerlifting lifts (squat, bench, deadlift) and is appropriate for both first-time structured cycle users and athletes returning after layoffs.

## Target Athlete Profile

Competitive powerlifters with established technique in all three lifts. Level II rank through Master of Sport, or equivalent competition results. Body weight minimum 60 kg with normal weight-to-height ratio.

The cycle requires an active lifestyle with proper recovery. It is unsuitable for athletes with demanding physical labor jobs because the prescribed volume assumes the athlete is recovering rather than depleting on non-training days. No acute injuries or significant musculoskeletal issues.

## Training Structure

Three sessions per week with at least one rest day between workouts. Twelve weeks, twelve microcycles. No competitions, max attempts, or program deviations during the cycle — the load math assumes uninterrupted progression.

The cycle is a strength-focused phase. Maximum six reps in main exercises, matching the strength-period rule used across the LMS strength cycles.

Each session includes four components: warm-up, main complex (the prescribed strength work for the day's lifts), cool-down (lighter loads at 30 to 60% of max performed with controlled tempo), and flexibility work (post-workout, prioritizing hamstrings, shoulders, and chest).

## Setup Instructions

Download cycle14.xlsm and enable Excel editing and macros. Review program details on the "INFO" sheet. Navigate to the "1 per day" sheet for the prescribed daily work. Set actual start dates and the weekly training day schedule, ensuring rest days between sessions. Input current 1RM values for squat, bench, and deadlift in the highlighted yellow cells using realistic values. Set the correction percentage: 0.5% for natural athletes, with a maximum of 1.5% recommended for natural athletes pushing aggressive progression. Begin training.

## Why a Separate Cycle for Lighter Weight Classes

The 80 kg minimum in most LMS cycles is not arbitrary — the load math, the prescribed accessory volumes, and the absolute intensities are calibrated for the recovery capacity of an 80 kg+ athlete. A 65 kg athlete running SRC1 will encounter different fatigue accumulation patterns because the relationship between absolute load, relative load, and tissue capacity differs at lighter body weights.

SRC14 recalibrates these relationships. The prescribed volumes scale to the lighter body class, the accessory work choices reflect the strength imbalances common at lighter weights (often more posterior chain emphasis because lighter lifters' technique tends to emphasize leverage over raw mass), and the intensity progression assumes the smaller absolute weights can be progressed more aggressively in absolute terms while remaining manageable in relative terms.

## Suitable Use Cases

SRC14 fits three athlete profiles. First, lighter-weight-class powerlifters at any level from II rank through Master of Sport who want a structured cycle calibrated for their body weight. Second, athletes returning from a layoff who have lost mass and now compete in a lighter class than their previous cycles assumed. Third, advanced lifters at heavier classes who want to drop to a lower class for competition and need a programming framework that accommodates the resulting body weight reduction.

## Post-Cycle Progression

After SRC14, the typical progression is either a competition, a peak-strength cycle (block cycles for powerlifters at the appropriate level), or another iteration of SRC14 with updated 1RM inputs. The cycle's wide level range means the athlete can rerun it across multiple training years, growing the absolute strength substantially while staying within the cycle's structural framework.

## Constraints to Respect

The 60 kg minimum body weight is not an arbitrary lower bound — it reflects where the LMS load calibration begins to break down. Athletes lighter than 60 kg should consult the cycle's documentation about further adaptation or work with a coach to design custom programming, because the cycle's load math assumptions have not been validated below that threshold.
