# Powerlifting Programming

## Squat Programming

The squat is the most technically demanding of the three powerlifts. Optimal frequency for strength development is 2-3 sessions per week. A common structure separates a heavy/competition squat day from a lighter technique or volume day.

Key technique cues for maximal squatting: brace the core 360 degrees before descent (Valsalva maneuver), push knees out tracking over toes, maintain a neutral spine with a slight forward lean appropriate to build proportions, drive hips up and back out of the hole. For raw powerlifters, a high-bar or low-bar position is chosen based on anatomy and leverages — low-bar generally allows more load due to reduced moment arm.

Accessory selection for squat: Romanian deadlifts and leg press for quad/glute volume, safety bar squats for variation and upper-back stability, pause squats to reinforce the hole position.

## Bench Press Programming

Bench press responds exceptionally well to high frequency — 3-4 sessions per week is supported by research and elite programming (Sheiko, Norwegian approaches). This is possible because the bench involves smaller muscle mass and recovers faster than the squat or deadlift.

Technical elements: retract and depress scapulae before unracking, maintain a stable leg drive, control the bar path to the lower chest/upper abdomen, drive the bar back and toward the face during the press (J-curve). Leg drive contributes 10-20% of force production for experienced lifters.

Accessories: close-grip bench for tricep overload, overhead press for shoulder health and general pressing strength, dumbbell flyes and rows for pec/upper back balance.

## Deadlift Programming

The deadlift is the most fatiguing lift systemically. Most successful programs train conventional or sumo deadlift heavy 1-2 times per week, supplemented by lighter Romanian deadlifts, deficit pulls, or block pulls.

Conventional technique: hip hinge to grip, lat activation ("protect your armpits"), push the floor away (not "pull the bar up"), lock out with glutes. Sumo technique: wide stance, vertical torso, knees pushed out, short range of motion leverages the hips.

Competition prep for deadlift: in the final 2-3 weeks, reduce volume significantly and prioritize speed pulls at 80-85% to build explosiveness without excessive fatigue.

## Competition Peaking

Standard competition peak structure (12-week prep):
- Weeks 1-8: GPP and volume accumulation (3-4 sets of 4-6 at 75-85%)
- Weeks 9-10: Intensity phase, reduce volume (3x3-4 at 85-90%)
- Weeks 11-12: Peak and taper (work up to opener-level singles at 92-95%)
- Competition day: Attempt selection — opener at ~90% of max, second at competition PR or near-max, third attempt at absolute max if second went well

## Opener Selection and Attempt Strategy

The opener should be a weight the athlete can hit on their worst day: approximately 90-92% of current training max. A missed opener is catastrophic; a successful opener sets the tone. Second attempts target the competitive PR or a personal record. Third attempts chase the absolute maximum — only attempt if the second lift was clean and fast.
