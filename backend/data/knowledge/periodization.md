# Periodization in Strength Training

## Linear Periodization

Linear periodization is the most straightforward model: volume decreases while intensity increases over successive training phases. A classic 12-week block might begin at 4x10 at 65% 1RM, progress to 4x6 at 75%, and peak at 4x3 at 85-90%. This approach suits beginners and early intermediates who respond well to a single, predictable stimulus. The limitation is monotony — advanced athletes adapt quickly and stall.

## Daily Undulating Periodization (DUP)

DUP alternates the training stimulus across sessions within the same week. For example: Monday is hypertrophy (4x10 at 65%), Wednesday is strength (5x5 at 80%), Friday is power (6x2 at 88%). This frequent variation maintains neural freshness and has been shown in research to produce greater strength gains than linear periodization over 8-12 weeks for intermediate lifters. DUP is particularly effective for athletes training 3-4 days per week.

## Block Periodization

Block periodization organizes training into sequential concentrated blocks, each lasting 3-6 weeks:

- **Accumulation block**: High volume (5x8-10), moderate intensity (60-70% 1RM), building work capacity and hypertrophy
- **Transmutation block**: Moderate volume (4x4-6), higher intensity (75-85% 1RM), converting hypertrophy to strength
- **Realization block**: Low volume (3x1-3), peak intensity (90-100% 1RM), expressing accumulated strength

Each block builds on the previous. This model suits advanced athletes who need concentrated doses of a specific quality before moving on.

## Volume and Intensity Waves

Wave loading organizes sets in ascending or descending intensity patterns to take advantage of post-activation potentiation. A classic ascending wave: 6 reps at 80%, 4 reps at 85%, 2 reps at 90% — then repeat the wave heavier. Research supports wave loading for maximizing peak force output in a single session.

Weekly volume should be progressively overloaded: add 1-2 sets or 2-5% load per week within a block. Deload every 4th week by reducing volume 40-50% while maintaining intensity.

## Peaking Protocols

A peaking phase typically runs 2-4 weeks before a competition or test. Key principles:

- Reduce training volume by 40-60% compared to peak accumulation
- Maintain or increase intensity (90%+ 1RM work)
- Increase specificity (practice competition movements with competition parameters)
- Allow 7-10 days of very low volume before the test date (taper)

A common 3-week peaking structure: Week 1 — 75% of normal volume at 85-90% intensity; Week 2 — 50% volume at 90-95%; Week 3 (competition week) — 25% volume, opener attempts only at 92-95%.
